Quickstart · 5 minutes

Box to live data, six steps.

Most installs are running before the kettle finishes. The wizard handles CAN bus and ECU detection automatically — your only real choice is which of the three Studio paths suits your garage.

  1. Wire 4 cables Red → +12 V switched · Black → ground · Green → CAN H · Yellow → CAN L
  2. Ignition on Dash boots straight into the First-Run Wizard.
  3. CAN auto-scan 125 / 250 / 500 / 1000 kbps — locks on in 2–4 s.
  4. Pick your ECU MaxxECU, Haltech, Ford, GM GEN IV, Custom — the lot.
  5. Connect option Home Wi-Fi, USB cable, or the dash's own hotspot.
  6. Tap Finish Live data flowing. Done.

Install firmware · OTA

Updates over Wi-Fi — no cables

Updates ship over Wi-Fi straight to the dash. The firmware keeps two slots and rolls back automatically if a new version fails to boot — you can't brick the device.

  1. Confirm Wi-Fi Dash needs to be on your home network with internet. Check Diagnostics → Wi-Fi card for "Internet: OK".
  2. Check Updates Device Settings → Network → Check Updates. The dash queries the update server and shows the new version + release notes if one's available.
  3. Download & Install Tap to start. Progress bar shows download (~2 MB), then the dash reboots into the new firmware. The whole thing takes about 30 seconds on decent Wi-Fi.
  4. Auto-check banner When idle on home Wi-Fi, the dash polls every few hours in the background. A small banner appears on the home screen if an update lands — tap to jump straight to the prompt.
  5. If it fails Failed downloads retry up to 3 times. Weak signal is the most common cause — move closer to your AP or use the desktop USB path as a fallback.
Wiring & CAN

Four wires, no soldering

The harness ships with a colour-coded pigtail. Match the colours to your ECU's CAN-bus wiring diagram (look for "CAN H" / "CAN L"), and you're done.

Red +12 V power · switched ignition source, not constant 12 V
Black Ground · clean chassis ground or battery negative
Green CAN High · ECU CAN-H (sometimes labelled CANH)
Yellow CAN Low · ECU CAN-L (sometimes labelled CANL)

Use twisted-pair cable for the green/yellow run for best signal integrity. CAN networks need a 120 Ω terminator at each end — most aftermarket ECUs already terminate at their end, and the RDM-7 has a built-in jumper on the rear cover (yellow block, bottom-right). Bridge the two pins to enable.

Custom / DBC
Manual CAN · DBC import

Don't see yours? The Custom preset lets you enter CAN IDs by hand (CAN ID, bit start, length, scale, offset, endianness), and Studio's DBC Import tool converts a vendor-supplied .dbc file into RDM signals in one shot. The Marketplace hosts community-contributed signal packs too — someone may have already done the work for your car.
